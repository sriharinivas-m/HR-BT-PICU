Heart Rate and Body Temperature Relationship in Children Admitted to PICU: A Machine Learning Approach

This project focuses on analyzing the relationship between heart rate and body temperature in children admitted to the Pediatric Intensive Care Unit (PICU) using machine learning approaches. These two vital signs are critical indicators that play a key role in identifying early signs of infection, fever spikes, sepsis, and other critical conditions. By applying advanced machine learning techniques including quantile regression and statistical analysis, the project evaluates patient data to uncover meaningful relationships and patterns between these vital signs.

The core objective of this project is to quantify and understand the relationship between heart rate and body temperature variations in PICU patients. By analyzing how heart rate responds to changes in body temperature across different age groups, medical conditions, and clinical scenarios, this project aims to enhance monitoring efficiency and support more accurate clinical decision-making. This innovative machine learning approach combines medical expertise with intelligent data analysis to improve patient safety and overall outcomes in pediatric critical care.
